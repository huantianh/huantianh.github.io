Research

My research interests are in control systems, time-delay systems, robotics, mechatronics, and engineering education.

My doctoral work focused on Lyapunov-Krasovskii based methods for time-delay systems, with emphasis on computational efficiency and feedback control.

Research Interests

Time-Delay Systems

Stability analysis and control of systems with delayed states, delayed inputs, and distributed delay terms.

Lyapunov-Krasovskii Methods

Development and implementation of Lyapunov-Krasovskii functionals for stability and feedback control problems.

Computational Control

Efficient numerical methods for solving control-related linear matrix inequalities and stability conditions.

Robotics and Mechatronics

Applications of control, sensing, actuation, and embedded systems in robotics and mechatronics education.

Engineering Education

Development of hands-on teaching materials, demonstrations, and active-learning activities for engineering students.

Doctoral Research

My dissertation is titled:

Lyapunov-Krasovskii Based Approach to Time-Delay Systems: Computational Efficiency and Feedback Control

This work studied computationally efficient approaches for stability analysis and feedback control of time-delay systems using Lyapunov-Krasovskii functionals.

Current Directions

  • Control systems simulations and classroom demonstrations
  • Computational methods for time-delay systems
